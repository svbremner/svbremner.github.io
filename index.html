About

I entered the PhD program at Columbia University in 2015, having earned an A.B. in Philosophy from the University of Chicago in 2014 and having completed a DAAD Research Fellowship at Goethe-Universität Frankfurt in 2015. I work primarily on Kant (particularly the Third Critique and practical philosophy), 20th century European philosophy (particularly Foucault and Beauvoir), and moral/social philosophy. Starting in 2021, I will be an assistant professor in the History of Philosophy department of the University of Groningen in the Netherlands.

My dissertation defends a Kantianism that espouses an ethics of principles while emphasizing the role of revising moral principles. It claims that the resources to account for moral revision can be found in Kant’s theory of reflective judgment, the cognitive power required for forming new concepts or principles on the basis of experience. Reflective judgment, on this account, involves both the incorporation of new moral particulars into a standing moral conceptual repertoire and the revision of moral universals on the basis of such alterations. The alteration of moral universals and accommodation of recalcitrant particulars are among the operations of what I term the ‘reflective dimension’ of practical reasoning, alongside the moral dimension of application of a priori moral principle (most notably, the categorical imperative) typically taken to exhaust Kant’s moral philosophy. I claim that exercising reflective judgment in practical reason calls for a critical stance to one’s principles in general, aligning the concerns of critical theory (in particular, genealogy critique) with those of Kantian ethics.

I have also written about Michel Foucault on normativity and critique, culture’s role in unifying the two parts of the Third Critique, J.L. Austin on the normativity of epistemic discursive claims, and autonomy in Kant’s Opus Postumum. I am currently thinking about contemporary art and Kant, and genealogy in Simone de Beauvoir. For more, see my Research page and CV.
